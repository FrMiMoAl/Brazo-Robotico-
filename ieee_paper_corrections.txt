2. METHODOLOGICAL REFOCUS (REVISED INTRODUCTION AND APPROACH)

Introduction
The precise manipulation of objects in unstructured environments remains a significant challenge in robotics. This paper presents a Position-Based Visual Servoing (PBVS) architecture for a 4-DOF robotic manipulator, utilizing an RGB-D sensor (Kinect v2) in an eye-to-hand configuration. By leveraging depth data and color-based object detection, the system calculates the 3D spatial coordinates of a target in real-time. The robust translation of these coordinates into the robot's base frame is critical for accurate trajectory generation. This study aims to evaluate the kinematic calibration and validate the resulting Cartesian positioning accuracy in a controlled laboratory environment, establishing a reliable foundation for autonomous pick-and-place operations.

Approach and Justification
To ensure precise spatial alignment between the perception system and the manipulator, an eye-to-hand static calibration approach was implemented. A target object was positioned across multiple known points within the robot's workspace to capture corresponding 3D coordinates in both the camera's optical frame and the robot's base frame. The Kabsch algorithm and physical geometric constraints were employed to calculate the optimal rigid-body transformation (rotation and translation) minimizing the Root Mean Square Deviation (RMSD). This PBVS approach allows the system to decouple the perception processing from the low-level joint control, providing a modular and scalable solution for vision-guided manipulation tasks.
